First Arrival#

Story in aɖasi#

Upon reaching aɖasi, then women have a rough time surviving in the wilds. The thinner atmosphere required time to acclimate to, and much of the flora was unknown to the women. While water was abundant, the primary problem was food. Yet seɹikani had sent the women to this planet because she had known the goddesses. It wasn’t long before the dormant goddesses, now known as the aiɖi, awakened because of the newcomers and contacted them.

There were 6 aiɖi on the planet during this time. The 4 original aiɖi of aɖasi, born and tied to the system of zai, were etiaʃi, aʃieti, iʈʂedy and iɖʐe. zɛɹa was an aiɖi of foreign origin, but was now an aiɖi of zai due to the millions of years she had spent here. seʃɹø, the last aiɖi, is the daughter of aʃieti and iʈʂedy.

ajɛseɖi met etiaʃi first in her dreams where the aiɖi helped her by showing her viable plants for her consumption. Eventually, etiaʃi explains the dilemma of the aiɖi in this system. Due to the laws of nature in zai, an aiɖi can only appear in the Material Plane when called with intention. Thus, the aiɖi had attempted many ways to produce an intelligent species with the ability to call the aiɖi to the Material Plane, and yet they were largely unsuccessful, instead populating the planet with different kinds of flora.

etiaʃi proposed that if ajɛseɖi and the other women helped her in making such viable creatures, she would help her in establishing a safe haven for her family and friends on aɖasi away from aθɶs. ajɛseɖi and the others agreed and etiaʃi started to train ajɛseɖi, saiʃeʈa and saeθa in the art of sʋe. etiaʃi’s sister, aʃieti did not agree with this decision and refused to help. On the other hand, zɛɹa and iɖʐe helped in training saiʃeʈa and saeθa, while ajɛseɖi herself was under the tutelage of etiaʃi. seʃɹø, caught between the different decisions of her parents as well as nature of creating, largely remained neutral in the training and creation process, but kept tabs on the progress of the women.

After a few months of training, etiaʃi and ajɛseɖi first initiated the creation of the zea ʈeaθyɖa as they decided to call them. ajɛseɖi modeled the race after her own body, but also applying several changes to the bodily functions and physical aspect. The creation process, which lasted 5 days and produced about 200 ʈeaθyɖa, was successful, but also changed ajɛseɖi[1].

The women, as well as the other aiɖi, monitored the fledgeling race for a year, noting any irregularities. In a years time, ajɛseɖi put down a proposal for two other genders which would accompany the ajɛsɖɶ in the areas that they lacked. Thus, saeθa with zɛɹa designed the ɖiʒiɖi, while saiʃeʈa with iɖʐe designed the esiʈi. The other genders were slowly incorporated into the ajɛsɖɶ communities. But the women strictly reduced any intervention in the early ʈeaθyɖa. At the time, their belief was to let the ʈeaθyɖa form their own communities. The aiɖi did imbue them with the memory of language on creation, but ideas such as community standards, ideals, and behavior was left to the ʈeaθyɖa.

We must also note some developments to the aiɖi population in zai. In parts of their studies to learn the sʋe, the women had to visit the Ethereal Realm. etiaʃi, due to her dormancy as well as her nature, requested ajɛseɖi to bed her. As a result of this relationship, ajɛseɖi gave birth to an aiɖi, θaœ, while etiaʃi gave birth to ʃɶða. But θaœ wasn’t the only aiɖi born during these 5 years in aɖasi. The aiɖi iʈʂedy foresaw the need for more aiɖi in the system, specifically hers, and requested saiʃeʈa and saeθa to provide her with some. iʈʂedy inseminated both saeθa and saiʃeʈa rather quickly, and returned to her dormancy. In some time, saiʃeʈa gave birth to dœʋa and saeθa to ʋaðœ[2].

After the birth of the 4 aiɖi, ajɛseɖi approached etiaʃi to return them back to aθɶs so they may return with their families. And thus, she sent ajɛseɖi, saeθa and saiʃeʈa back to aθɶs.

Story in aθɶs#

ajɛseɖi, saeθa and saiʃeʈa disappear after the attack on the aɹinka. stɹai and eɹaʈɛ believe that they are dead and mourn their loss. stɹai adopts θisœʈe, dea and θea, while eɹaʈɛ adopts saeθa’s children. The ʋeʃjœ soldiers ravage aɹinka leaving little behind. eɹaʈɛ, being a ʋeʃja having no master since the Temple of the Primal Weaver has been destroyed, is taken back to ʋeʃjœ. stɹai opts not to return to ʋeʃjœ with eɹaʈɛ because of the volatile nature of the city, and remains in the country side. She makes her living off of working as a maid, or doing odd jobs. She is careful in returning back to prostitution no matter how desperate on a bid to remain ever faithful to ajɛseɖi.

eɹaʈɛ is bought by a noble man in ʋeʃjœ and she is kept in hopes that she gives birth to a child the wife of the noble man could never give. After 8 years of failure[3], the noble man throws eɹaʈɛ out and she returns to the Academy as an instructor. In 2 more years, seθi joins the Academy as a ʋeʃja’s daughter. seθi, while conditioned for her time in the Academy, still has a rough time in the Academy, especially as a Novice, studying fine arts. As she moves up to become a Trainee and then Accepted, she becomes much more comfortable, and grows to become an excellent ʋeʃja.

aʃaða joins the Academy as a Novice, when seθi is a Trainee, and thus is put in her charge. It may seem a little incestuous, but physical relationship between Novice and Trainee are much milder than those between a Trainee and Accepted. This pair up automatically avoided when seθi is an Accepted[4]. seθi is about to become a full ʋeʃja and teʃi is about to be taken to the Academy when ajɛseɖi, saeθa and saiʃeʈa reappear to bring their partners and children to aɖasi.

As we briefly mentioned before, stɹai works a life of labor in a nearby city primarily filled with refugees from aɹinka. She works odd jobs initially, but begins her own bakery at a point in her life. stɹai has it particularly rough as she is the target of many rumors, being the sole caretaker of θisœʈe, dea and θea. While most believe that θisœʈe is stɹai’s sister, or even half-sister, they are convinced that dea and θea are born out of wedlock. Most women avoid her, and frequently soldiers and bankers would force her. Despite stɹai’s insistence, θisœʈe chooses not to marry, having grown a disgust of men from their treatment of stɹai, and eventually herself. As such, θisœʈe starts spending time with the only women who will talk with her: those in the unreputed district.

Despite disobeying many of stɹai’s words, θisœʈe is still close to stɹai. And at some point in their life, θisœʈe becomes physically attracted to stɹai despite their difference in age. θisœʈe herself is confused by these thoughts and is hesitant to tell her.

stɹai’s life becomes a little stable, as with her growing age, fewer men made advances on her and left her alone[5]. Having grown content in her own solitude and providing for the twins, stɹai slowly grows happier. This is when ajɛseɖi appears again, throwing stɹai’s now steady life back into turbulence.

In latter years, he visited local apothecaries for herbs to enhance his semen production. It is an irony that eɹaʈɛ having adopted saeθa’s children was bought by the noble man because of his thought that she was extremely fertile (the 3 being fairly close together in age). eɹaʈɛ on her part never revealed that seθi, aʃaða and teʃi were not her children, thinking the noble man would throw them away. At the same time, eɹaʈɛ was treated rather horribly by the wife of the noble man who out of jealousy was horrible to her.

At the same time, the definition of incest is limited to close relationships, such as sisters, and half-sisters (those who share one or both parents), but not cousins.

[5]

One can ask, if people made less advances against stɹai, then why did they not make them at the younger θisœʈe. θisœʈe had grown a reputation as a racuous, indulgent, and not particularly feminine woman. Thus most men, if not people, avoided her. Besides working with stɹai at the bakery, she would spend her time outside, drinking with the prostitutes, or playing with their children.
