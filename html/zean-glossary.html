Footnotes

[1]

Some of the English definitions offered in this lexicon are mere approximations of their actual meaning. Some of these words may be employed in a manner that does not coincide with the English meaning, but in terms of the philosophy of the language make better sense.

[2]

Informally, negation of something is usually the same word but starting with ʐ if the syllabic rules allow.

[3]

Which of the two definitions to use can be guessed using context

[4]

The second statement and statements of a similar style are used as a metaphor and aren’t taken literally.

[5]

aθɶs while commonly used in the Zean language isn’t a Zean word, as it plainly disregards the rules of creating syllables in the Zean language. Thus it and all words originating from aθɶs (besides the names of the isiʈeθɶ and zœeɖaʃi) are included in a separate section.
